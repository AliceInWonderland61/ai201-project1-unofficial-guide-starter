Do keep in mind that some of these classes are conceptually easier than others but are earlier in the curriculum and therefore are relatively more difficult. Also, the quality of the prof will influence the difficulty; I had a bad prof for both PSoft and Proglang and it seriously detracted from the course quality. With that said, here's my ranking:  

1. Data structures (largest time commitment of all CS classes, and you're introduced to a whole bunch of difficult new concepts)
2. FOCS (a whole bunch of brand new concepts and also a fairly high time commitment)
3. Proglang (some of the languages there actually gave me a headache)
4. Algo (conceptually difficult)
5. Opsys (conceptually somewhat tough and reasonable workload; this course is also often taken over Arch which doesn't help)
6. PSoft (concepts aren't too hard and relatively moderate time commitment)
7. Comp Org (conceptually pretty easy and low time commitment)

I came in with AP credit for CS1 so I never took it here and can't rank it.

For reference, I'm a second-semester sophomore (no OpSys or ProgLang yet) who came into the school with prior programming experience. I also won't be commenting on Algos and PSoft because I'm taking them this semester.

&#x200B;

Ranked on difficulty:

* FOCS (Malik) - Honestly, I struggled through this class. Malik is a crazy smart guy (he's the prof and wrote the textbook) and wants to share his passion with all of us. However, while his lessons were (almost) always clear, the tests and exams kicked my ass.
* Comp Org (Kuzmin)- When I took the class last semester we had an exam every other week, and while it was open notes, for most of the class we were writing MIPS Assembly and that shit's hard AF to write on paper. The concepts were challenging at times, but things never didn't make sense after some thought - and MIPS will take a **lot** of thought for most people.
* Data Structures (Plum)- The concepts aren't hard, but expect to spend more time on this class than you thought possible. Learn from me: don't take 20 credits during DS semester...
* CS1 (Mushtaque)- If you come in with a basic understanding of programming, you'll be fine.

&#x200B;

Ranked on time commitment:

* Data Structures (Plum)- I pulled probably half a dozen all-nighters for this class. I'm not a bad programmer by any means and I understood the course content, however, the homework **will** probably take you all week. Tip: Work on the homework the moment it's released.
* Comp Org (Kuzmin)- Make sure you stay sharp with your C/C++ from DS and you'll be fine for the first portion of the class. For the MIPS portion (the majority of the semester), make sure you have good reference sheets and learn the language as fast as possible.
* FOCS (Malik)- Homeworks and proofs just took me a lot of time to conceptualize. I spent a lot more time on the homework than most people I know.
* CS1 (Mushtaque)- I already knew Python, so the homeworks usually didn't take me more than a couple of hours. I know people who spent a dozen or so hours on the homeworks, but they were people who didn't know Python coming into the course. I finished the final project in a single night.
